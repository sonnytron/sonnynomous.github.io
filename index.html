About Me

Born in Southern California to a minority family, as a first generation college student, I faced a lot of challenges during my adolescency and maturing adulthood.\n When I entered college, I knew teachers had called me "very smart, but not motivated" but I had no idea how far that could go if I applied effort. After spending a few years repairing remedial education and studies in math and literacy, I was accepted into University of California, San Diego's Engineering School for Environmental Engineering. Upon graduation, I quickly realized that today's problems aren't so easily solved with the physical engineering science, as resources and energy are quickly becoming scarce and organizations and governments aren't keen to invest into mechanical and chemical engineering necessary to solve climate problems. I still wanted to help people, but I retroactively adapted my discipline to computer science with an emphasis on Software Development Engineering. The path toward becoming a Software Engineer was long, harsh and full of setbacks. But I had a lot of experience with setbacks. I had a lot of experience with rejection and failure. So I kept rowing. Now I live in Fukuoka Japan and I work for one of the largest social networking technology companies in the world. Where will I be next? Let's find out.
